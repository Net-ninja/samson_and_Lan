Haile Selassie

The Lion of Judah

  • Haile Selassie was born Tafari Makonnen on July 23, 1892, in Ethiopia.
  • He was a prominent African leader who served as the Emperor of Ethiopia from 1930 to 1974.
  • Haile Selassie was a key figure in Africa's struggle for independence and was known for his efforts to modernize Ethiopia and improve the lives of its people.
  • He was a devout Christian and believed in the importance of education, establishing schools and universities throughout Ethiopia.
  • Haile Selassie was a strong advocate for African unity and played a leading role in the formation of the Organization of African Unity (OAU), which later became the African Union (AU).
  • He was also a vocal opponent of colonialism and played a key role in the decolonization of Africa.
  • Haile Selassie is also revered by Rastafarians, who believe that he is a messianic figure and the embodiment of God on Earth.
  • Despite his achievements, Haile Selassie's reign was not without controversy, and he faced criticism for his authoritarian rule and handling of certain domestic and international issues.
  • He was deposed in a coup in 1974 and died under mysterious circumstances in 1975.
  • Haile Selassie remains an important figure in Ethiopian and African history, and his legacy continues to inspire people around the world.
